About Daman

Daman is popular tourist destination in India, known for its Portuguese heritage and serene beaches. the city is divided into two parts by the Daman Ganga River: Nani-Damn and Moti-Daman.

Nani-Daman is the larger of the two parts, while old city is mainly in Moti-Daman. The city has a tropical savanna climate with to distinct seasons: a long, sunny dry season form October to May  and a hot, very humid, and extremely wet monsoon season from June to September.

Tourists can explore several quaint churches and ancient forts that exude sn old-world charm and are fine specimens of the Portuguese style of architecture. The Modi Daman Fort is popular tourist attraction, and there are several beaches where visitors can relax and enjoy the scenery.

Some of the popular tourist attractions in Daman are Ghoghla Beach, Jallandhar beach, Gangeshwar Temple, St. Paul's Church, Mirasol Lake Garden, Jampore Beach, Bom Jesus Church, and Somnath Temple.

Daman is connected to other cities by the Mumbai-Ahmedabad highway via Vapi Road, and there are regular bus services from major cities, including Mumbai, Surat, Ahmedabad, Udaipur, Nasik, and Shirdi. The nearest airport to Daman is the Mumbai Airport, which is qabout 170 km away.

In conclusion, Daman is a beautiful city with a rich history and culture that attracts tourists from all over the world. Visitors can explore the city's Portuguese heritage, relax on the serene beaches, and enjoy the local cuisine.
